Music Recommendation System¶

Milestone 1¶

Problem Definition¶

The context: Why is this problem important to solve?

  • According to Spotify, 60,000 new tracks are now being ingested by its platform every single day and 22 million tracks are generated per year through out the world. At the sametime, there are millions of people who use variety of apps to listen music and have a variety of taste in music.
  • Different songs have different genre and mood, and people would also prefer to listen music depending on their mood.
  • With such a variety if music existing, and given choice of each user, it is manually impossible to identify likes/dislikes of each user and recommend them songs.
  • Recommending right kind of songs to right kind of users, would develop a trust and satisfaction in the hearts of users for our website/application, thus ensuring customer loyalty.
  • This will bring more business and growth for the website/app.
  • Providing personalized song recommendation service to the users would attract more customer

The objectives: What is the intended goal?

  • The intended goal can be interpreted in 2 Ways:
    1. From User's Perspective: To find similar songs to their likings
    2. From Application's (Company's) Perspective: To attract more customers and increase their revenue by providing personalized recommendations.

Apart from this, few other primary goals of recommendation systems are:

  • To provide personalized recommendation, and not only recommending most popular songs.
  • To retain the customers (listeners)

The key questions: What are the key questions that need to be answered?

  • Which of the types of recommendation system performs well on this data? (In real-life, this question can be resovled by performing A/B testing)
  • Which are the most popular songs, artists, and albums?
  • Which are the most frequent users? Can these customers be granted some kind of offers, to retain them forever/ for a longer time?
  • How to recommend songs to the users who have listened/ listen to very few songs? How to increase their interactivity?

The problem formulation: What are we trying to solve using data science?

  • Here, we're trying to sovle a retail business problem of matching user's preferences with right type of song.
  • In this case, we can say, we are focusing on Personalization of service, to attract more customers and inturn increase reveneu.

Data Dictionary¶

The core data is the Taste Profile Subset released by the Echo Nest as part of the Million Song Dataset. There are two files in this dataset. The first file contains the details about the song id, titles, release, artist name, and the year of release. The second file contains the user id, song id, and the play count of users.

song_data

song_id - A unique id given to every song

title - Title of the song

Release - Name of the released album

Artist_name - Name of the artist

year - Year of release

count_data

user _id - A unique id given to the user

song_id - A unique id given to the song

play_count - Number of times the song was played

Think About It: As the user_id and song_id are encrypted. Can they be encoded to numeric features?

  • user_id and song_id are encrypted, which makes them categorical features.
  • Categorical features can be converted into numeric features using Label Encoding. One drawback of Label Encoding is that this method assumes an Ordinal Relationship between the values of these features (like eg. 1>2>3 and so on).
  • But in our usecase, this features won't be used for any numeric interpretation. In fact converting into numeric features will make them more interpretable and simple.Hence they can be converted into numeric features using Label Encoding Method!

Think About It: As the data also contains users who have listened to very few songs and vice versa, is it required to filter the data so that it contains users who have listened to a good count of songs and vice versa?

  • Yes, for users which have listened to very few songs, it would be difficult to know about their choice of songs, and in the distance/similarity matrix calculation, there won't be any substantial values available for such users.
  • Hence, we can filter data in a way that users who have listened to a certain amount of songs are onl considered, and in fact users who have heard very few songs, can be recommended songs similar to their choice.
  • Now, deciding, the minimum number of songs which users should have heard, (eg 90) or another number is a hyperparameter which we can tune & optimize!
  • Similarly, for the songs, here we are considering those songs, which at least 120 users have heard. Again, this number can be tuned according to the accuracy.

Proposed approach¶

Potential techniques: What different techniques should be explored?

Now, data can be preprocessed based on play_count column in 2 ways.¶

    1. Filtering data based on count (i.e. occurence of values of play_count)
    1. Normalizing the play_count values in the range of 1-10 using MinMaxScaler()
1. Filtering data based on count of play_count.¶
  • In the above pre-processing task, we dropped records with play_count>5. But this can lead to unnecessary loss of information. For example, a play_count of 10 would indicate that a specific user has heard this particular song for 10 times, and it is his favourite!. In the given approach, this information would be lost.
  • So a solution to this is, considering all the play_count record values, who have occured atleast 5 times! Filtering implemented below.
  • Visualizing distribution of play_count
  • It can be seen that there are very few records, where users have heard a particular song for thousands of times.
  • A good idea would be to consider these as outliers, and not include them in our recommendation system.
  • For this, we can consider all the play_count values, which have occured for atleast 5 times in our interaction dataset.
  • Again 5 is an hyper parameter which can be tuned.

Overall solution design: What is the potential solution design?

  • There are 3 different ways as shown above to pre-process the data and further, each of these data pre-processing methods can be used to implement following 3 types of recommendation systems algorithms
    1. Rank Based Recommendation System
    1. Collaborative Filtering Method a. User-User Based Similarity
      b. Song-Song Based Similarity
    1. Model based Recommendation System using Matrix Factorization (SVD)
    1. Graph Neural Networks (Optional)

Measures of success: What are the key measures of success to compare different potential technqiues?

  • The key measures of success to compare above potential technique would be as follows:
    1. Acuracy (R-squared, RMSE value)
      • The model would predict the play_count for the given users, based on KNN algorithm and similarity. This can be measured by using R-squared, RMSE, MAPE measures of success as play_count is a numeric quantity.
    1. Precision
      • Depending on the range of play_count, I would decide a threshold to identify relevant items, for example, if I consider range of play_count as 1-10, then cut-off threshold would be around 5. Any predicted value above 5, that particular song, would be considered a potential song for recommendation.
      • Precision would correctly represent, out of recommended songs, which songs were actually heard by the users. So in this usecase, precision would be more useful.
      • Although, we can't completely ignore recall, as it would correctly represent the proportion of songs which were correctly recommended out of relevant songs. In such a case, we can strike a balance between Recall & Precision by using F1 score too
